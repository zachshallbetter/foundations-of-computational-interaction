# Computational Interaction Science — Version 2.0 Research Program

## Status

Non-normative research agenda. Nothing in this directory modifies Version 1.0 definitions, ontology, contracts, or formal semantics.

## Scientific threshold

Version 1.0 is a coherent and formalizable interdisciplinary synthesis. Version 2.0 must determine whether its distinctions produce reliable, valid, causal, predictive, or intervention-relevant gains sufficient to justify their complexity.

## Workstream 1 — Cross-paper consistency and canonical registry

Maintain one stable term ID, current label, canonical definition, status, dependencies, superseded definitions, forbidden synonyms, version history, validation status, and supporting or failed studies. Before formal revision, test symbol consistency, contract compatibility, participant scope, boundary assumptions, trace segmentation, uncertainty representation, and construct coverage.

## Workstream 2 — Boundary, identity, and participation

Develop principled tests for interaction attempts, coupling events, interaction episodes, recurrent interactions, and interaction systems. Specify episode initiation, suspension, resumption, nesting, splitting, termination, asynchronous closure, and multi-device continuation. Establish a participant admission test that distinguishes participants from passive resources and environmental conditions.

## Workstream 3 — Construct validation and contract ablation

Validate opportunity profiles, believed action sets, interpretive states, interaction strategies, interactional equivalence, and behavioral evidence quality. Measure inter-rater and test–retest reliability, convergent and discriminant validity, sensitivity to manipulation, calibration, stability, and failure conditions. Ablate contract fields to determine whether each distinction provides measurable explanatory or predictive gain.

## Workstream 4 — Causal architecture

Distinguish definitional counterfactual dependence, mechanistic dependence, statistical association, and intervention-supported causation. Specify causal graphs, confounders, mediators, moderators, interventions, identification assumptions, estimands, learning, carryover, and participant interference.

## Workstream 5 — Continuous and distributed interaction

Evaluate hybrid dynamics, continuous time, concurrency, partial observability, weak segmentation, embodied trajectories, ambient systems, institutional processes, and distributed coordination without forcing all phenomena into discrete event logs.

## Workstream 6 — Strategy and transfer

Define strategy identity independently of transfer outcomes. Preregister strategy invariants, admissible parameter changes, tactic substitutions, adaptation criteria, and replacement criteria before observing target-task performance.

## Workstream 7 — Culture and institutional mediation

Model conventions, roles, norms, authority, sanctions, practices, negotiated meaning, institutional memory, and division of labor as mechanisms without demographic essentialism. Expand engagement with activity theory, ethnomethodology, conversation analysis, practice theory, anthropology, and organizational studies.

## Workstream 8 — Adversarial evaluation independent of Fundamental

Test improvised social interaction, contested meaning, changing ontology, distributed authority, continuous coordination, ambiguous boundaries, asynchronous closure, unstable semantic state, and nondeterministic interpretation. Fundamental is an evaluation target, not an ontology source.

## Workstream 9 — Comparative scientific gain

Compare transition-system, HCI/cognitive-science, process-mining, full CompInt, and ablated CompInt accounts. Evaluate annotation agreement, construct recovery, explanatory fit, held-out prediction, intervention prediction, transfer prediction, cost, complexity, reporting burden, error detection, and decision utility.

## Conformance profiles to evaluate

- Minimum reporting core
- Comparative study profile
- Latent-inference profile
- Causal study profile
- Full foundational profile

## Decision rule

A construct or contract field advances only when evidence shows that it improves reliability, validity, explanation, prediction, intervention, or scientific coordination enough to justify its operational cost. Conceptual completeness alone is insufficient.
