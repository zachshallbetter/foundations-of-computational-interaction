# Scientific audit dimensions

## Scoring scale

| Score | Interpretation |
|---:|---|
| 0 | Absent, incoherent, or invalid |
| 1 | Materially deficient |
| 2 | Partially adequate |
| 3 | Scientifically adequate |
| 4 | Strong, explicit, and well-supported |

A score of 3 represents adequacy, not excellence. A score of 4 requires inspectable evidence exceeding the minimum standard.

## D1 - Problem significance

Evaluate whether the project addresses a real scientific problem whose resolution changes what can be described, tested, measured, explained, predicted, or compared.

Inspect:

- problem definition;
- scientific importance;
- gap evidence;
- scope;
- affected decisions;
- relationship to existing work.

## D2 - Contribution clarity

Evaluate whether the project makes one identifiable primary contribution and distinguishes it from motivation, implementation, demonstration, and application.

Inspect:

- one-sentence contribution;
- novelty boundary;
- dependency on prior work;
- claims of priority;
- contribution type;
- exclusions.

## D3 - Ontological coherence

Evaluate whether entities, relations, states, events, boundaries, identity conditions, levels, and exclusions are explicit and mutually coherent.

Inspect:

- category boundaries;
- identity and persistence;
- part-whole relations;
- levels of analysis;
- mutually exclusive and overlapping classes;
- undefined or overloaded entities.

## D4 - Semantic and definitional precision

Evaluate whether key terms are non-circular, stable, scoped, and distinguishable from neighboring concepts.

Inspect:

- definitions;
- necessary and sufficient conditions;
- synonym control;
- examples and counterexamples;
- scope conditions;
- prohibited substitutions.

## D5 - Formal and representational adequacy

Evaluate whether representations preserve the properties required by the claims and whether formal systems are internally valid.

Inspect:

- primitives and symbols;
- syntax and semantics;
- transformations;
- invariants;
- information loss;
- completeness relative to scope;
- consistency;
- edge cases;
- undefined states.

## D6 - Operationalization and measurement validity

Evaluate whether each construct is connected to a defensible observation and measurement process.

Inspect:

- construct;
- measurand;
- unit;
- level of analysis;
- observation model;
- instrument;
- coding;
- estimator;
- scale type;
- uncertainty;
- reliability;
- validity;
- invariance;
- missingness;
- prohibited interpretations.

## D7 - Design and identification

Evaluate whether the project design can distinguish the target claim from competing explanations.

For empirical projects, inspect:

- sampling;
- assignment;
- comparison conditions;
- confounding;
- mediation;
- moderation;
- interference;
- carryover;
- identification assumptions;
- stopping and exclusion rules.

For conceptual projects, inspect:

- premise selection;
- counterexamples;
- alternative frameworks;
- derivational validity;
- scope restrictions;
- discriminating consequences.

## D8 - Evidence quality

Evaluate relevance, provenance, directness, verification, independence, completeness, and convergence of evidence.

Inspect:

- raw sources;
- source authority;
- coding decisions;
- negative and contradictory evidence;
- triangulation;
- selective reporting;
- evidence-to-claim traceability.

## D9 - Analysis and uncertainty

Evaluate whether analysis methods match the estimand and whether uncertainty is represented adequately.

Inspect:

- estimand;
- estimator;
- assumptions;
- effect size;
- uncertainty interval;
- model diagnostics;
- robustness;
- sensitivity;
- multiplicity;
- missing data;
- null and ambiguous results.

## D10 - Comparative scientific gain

Evaluate whether the contribution improves scientific work relative to simpler or established alternatives.

Inspect:

- baseline selection;
- ablation;
- incremental validity;
- explanatory discrimination;
- predictive gain;
- transfer;
- error detection;
- coordination benefit;
- complexity cost.

## D11 - Reproducibility and cumulative utility

Evaluate whether another researcher can reconstruct the work and whether the result can participate in cumulative comparison.

Inspect:

- protocols;
- provenance;
- versioning;
- canonical identifiers;
- data and code availability;
- instrument specifications;
- contract conformance;
- stable reporting fields;
- comparability across studies.

## D12 - Explanatory restraint and conclusion validity

Evaluate whether conclusions remain within the actual evidence and distinguish description, explanation, prediction, intervention, and causation.

Inspect:

- scope discipline;
- alternative interpretations;
- causal language;
- generalization;
- practical significance;
- limitations;
- unsupported implications;
- recommendation warrants.
