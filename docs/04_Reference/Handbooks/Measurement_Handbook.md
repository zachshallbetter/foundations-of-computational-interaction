# Measurement Handbook

## Governing rule

A metric is not a construct.

## Measurement contract

For every measurement declare: construct, measurand, unit, level of analysis, observation model, instrument, operationalization, coding, estimator, scale type, permissible transformations, uncertainty, reliability, validity evidence, comparison population, task, projection, context, and prohibited interpretations.

## Evidence levels

1. Raw record.
2. Verified observation.
3. Coded observation.
4. Derived measure.
5. Model-based estimate.
6. Construct evidence.
7. Mechanism evidence.
8. Generalized claim.

## Canonical metric families

Timing; frequency; sequence; path; accuracy; completion; discovery; belief calibration; opportunity mismatch; recovery; reversibility; strategy diversity; transfer; prediction calibration; accessibility; measurement quality.

## Quality checks

Instrument calibration; inter-rater reliability; test-retest reliability; construct validity; convergent/discriminant evidence; measurement invariance; sensitivity analysis; missingness analysis; uncertainty budget; preregistered equivalence bounds.
