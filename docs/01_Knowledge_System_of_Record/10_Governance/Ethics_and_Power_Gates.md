# Ethics and Power Gates 0.1.0

These gates are non-compensatory: a favorable aggregate score cannot offset a
failed gate.

| Gate | Pass condition | Required record |
|---|---|---|
| coercion | refusal and withdrawal are practically available without disproportionate penalty | recruitment and withdrawal review |
| manipulation | influence mechanisms, deception, and debriefing are reviewed and bounded | manipulation inventory |
| exclusion and accessibility | required participants can access the task or exclusions are justified and reported | accessibility and exclusion audit |
| authority and institutional power | permissions, decision rights, and appeal paths are explicit | authority map |
| asymmetric risk | burdens and irreversible harms are not shifted onto less powerful groups | distributional risk review |
| subgroup preservation | no aggregate decision hides a failed subgroup gate | subgroup evidence record |
| cultural claim | context provenance is recorded and essentialist inference is prohibited | community review or limitation statement |
| reversibility | irreversible consequences have prevention, escalation, and remedy | harm and recovery plan |

Any failed or unassessed applicable gate vetoes a comparative recommendation,
deployment claim, or public release until the responsible review authority
records a resolution. The Comparison Contract must reference this record when
human or community consequences are in scope.
