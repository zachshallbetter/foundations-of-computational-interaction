# Deliverable 11: Ten-Year Research Roadmap

**Program:** Computational Interaction Science (CompInt)  
**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Status:** Canonical Reference Model  

This document outlines the milestones, institutional models, and empirical horizons for the Computational Interaction Science (CompInt) research program over the next ten years.

---

## 1. Timeline and Milestones

### Phase I: Foundation & Standardization (Years 1–2)
*   **Goal:** Solidify the contract stack and verify the reference substrate.
*   **Milestones:**
    *   *Year 1 (Q2):* Complete the Phase 0 consolidation, freezing the glossary and relational ontology.
    *   *Year 1 (Q4):* Finalize the draft contract schemas (version 1.0.0-normative) for Projection, Opportunity, and Measurement.
    *   *Year 2 (Q2):* Release the C reference kernel (`libcis` v1.0) with zero external symbols.
    *   *Year 2 (Q4):* Run the first controlled experimental pilot (Meal Selection Study) evaluating the Opportunity Mismatch Predictor.

### Phase II: Empirical Validation & Tooling (Years 3–5)
*   **Goal:** Deploy the Observatory platform, collect longitudinal traces, and test prediction models.
*   **Milestones:**
    *   *Year 3 (Q2):* Preregister and launch the first large-scale multi-representational study ($N > 1000$).
    *   *Year 3 (Q4):* Release the Observatory browser-extension logging tool, enabling trace capture in wild web habitats.
    *   *Year 4 (Q2):* Verify measurement invariance of the Opportunity Profile across mobile and desktop devices.
    *   *Year 4 (Q4):* Conduct the first formal study on **Interaction Grammar Transfer** comparing layout vs. syntax adaptation.
    *   *Year 5 (Q4):* Publish the first validated predictive models showing strategy selection based on prior exposure history.

### Phase III: Institutional Expansion & Open Standards (Years 6–7)
*   **Goal:** Integrate CompInt standards into software frameworks and establish university consortia.
*   **Milestones:**
    *   *Year 6 (Q2):* Release open-source middleware wrappers (e.g., React and Web components) that automatically output Projection and Opportunity Contracts.
    *   *Year 6 (Q4):* Establish the Joint Consortium on Interaction Research (JCIR) to coordinate study repositories.
    *   *Year 7 (Q4):* Conduct the first independent multi-site replication study verifying the Invariance Transfer Hypothesis across three continents.

### Phase IV: Disciplinary Synthesis & Final Determination (Years 8–10)
*   **Goal:** Evaluate the cumulative empirical record and resolve the disciplinary autonomy question.
*   **Milestones:**
    *   *Year 8 (Q4):* Publish the *Handbook of Computational Interaction Measurement* (consolidating all validation studies).
    *   *Year 9 (Q2):* Run a meta-analysis on 50+ studies to test the predictive superiority of CompInt over legacy cognitive models.
    *   *Year 10 (Q4):* Publish the final synthesis paper. Convene the JCIR to vote on whether CompInt merits recognition as an independent academic discipline, or if its formal models should remain integrated within computer science and psychology.

---

## 2. Institutional Models

*   **Open-Science Consortium:** Research must be coordinated through an open-science model. All study protocols, schemas, reference kernels, and anonymized datasets must be published to a centralized, public registry.
*   **Federated Data Storage:** To protect participant privacy and respect local data sovereignty, study data will be stored in decentralized repositories owned by the participating institutions rather than a single monolithic cloud database.
*   **Community-Led Ethics Boards:** Studies affecting specific groups or local nations must be overseen by ethics review boards containing active representatives from those communities, ensuring OCAP and CARE principles are enforced.

---

## 3. Empirical Horizons
The long-term success of the roadmap relies on advancing two major empirical questions:
1.  **Macro-Interaction Invariance:** Can we discover interaction grammars that remain completely invariant across radically different physical mediums (e.g., visual screens, spatial virtual reality, and haptic-acoustic devices)?
2.  **Autonomous Adaptation Limits:** Can predictive opportunity models help systems automatically adjust their visual complexity (morphology) to match a user's local fatigue, cognitive load, and accessibility needs in real time, without creating manipulative dark patterns?
