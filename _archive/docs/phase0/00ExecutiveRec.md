# Deliverable 00: Executive Recommendation

**Program Name:** Computational Interaction Science (CompInt)  
**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Status:** Canonical Determination Freeze  

---

## 1. Disciplinary Decision
**Recommendation:** **merge** (into an interdisciplinary scientific synthesis and research program, not an independent scientific discipline).

Computational Interaction Science is not presently justified as an autonomous or independent scientific discipline. It lacks unique, non-reducible physical mechanisms, autonomous empirical methods, and independent predictive models that cannot be represented within adjacent fields. However, as an *interdisciplinary synthesis*, CompInt provides immense coordinating value. It should be merged into existing academic and research structures (such as Human-Computer Interaction, Cognitive Science, and Computer Science) as a formal translation and coordination framework rather than defended as a new sovereign department.

---

## 2. Does the Field Deserve Existence?
**Yes, but strictly as an interdisciplinary research program, not an autonomous science.**

The core justification for CompInt’s existence is the coordination of highly fragmented concepts across computer science, psychology, design, and human factors. Currently, studies of interaction suffer from a lack of comparative precision: researchers conflate visual representation changes with semantic model changes, or treat telemetry click-logs as direct evidence of internal cognitive states. By separating these variables into a structured relational stack, CompInt offers:
1. **Analytical Disambiguation:** Keeping domain commitments, system state, projections, representations, opportunities, and strategies conceptually and computationally distinct.
2. **Methodological Comparability:** Providing structured, reusable contracts (Projection, Opportunity, Interaction, Behavioral Evidence, Measurement, Similarity, Strategy, and Prediction Contracts) that allow different studies to align their variables and metrics.
3. **Cumulative Coordination:** Coordinating cumulative research findings across projects and environments (such as the Fundamental substrate and the Observatory platform).

If evaluated as a sovereign discipline, CompInt fails the test of explanatory necessity. If evaluated as a coordinating interdisciplinary synthesis program, it solves a major replication and translation crisis in interaction research.

---

## 3. Strongest Arguments FOR the Research Program
*   **Separation of Confounds:** CompInt enforces a strict conceptual and computational separation between the system's underlying state/semantics and its user-facing projection. This allows researchers to isolate whether behavioral variances are caused by interface aesthetics (morphology), control layouts (exposure), or underlying logical state transitions.
*   **Formal Contract Infrastructure:** The introduction of explicit contract schemas (Projection, Opportunity, Measurement, etc.) provides a formal vocabulary for declaring research variables, making assumptions inspectable, and preventing overclaiming.
*   **Substrate Independence:** By modeling interaction as a relational episode involving coupled transition systems, the theoretical models remain portable across modalities, avoiding vendor-lock or hardware-specific paradigms.

---

## 4. Strongest Arguments AGAINST the Research Program
*   **Lack of Unique Primitives:** Every proposed concept in CompInt can be traced back to existing, well-developed concepts in adjacent fields. For instance, "Opportunity" is a coordination of affordances (ecological psychology), permissions (security/access control), and enablement (state transition systems).
*   **Overlapping Explanatory Territory:** Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Cognitive Psychology, Activity Theory, and Distributed Cognition already jointly cover the entire explanatory space of interaction. CompInt runs the risk of introducing a parallel vocabulary (branding) rather than discovering new phenomena.
*   **Unproven Empirical Superiority:** There is no longitudinal empirical evidence demonstrating that modeling interaction via the CompInt contract stack yields superior predictions of human behavior compared to established cognitive architectures (e.g., GOMS, ACT-R) or deep learning telemetry models.

---

## 5. Recommended Scope
The program should focus on:
1. **Developing and validating the contract stack** as a set of methodological standards for empirical research.
2. **Investigating interaction grammar transfer** and how exposure to specific software habitats shapes user expectations across unfamiliar interfaces.
3. **Providing a deterministic reference substrate (Fundamental)** to verify formal invariants before running empirical studies.

It must explicitly exclude:
*   Formulating an independent metaphysical ontology of human mind or society.
*   Making deterministic claims about identity, culture, or demographics without exposing specific exposure variables.
*   Inferring latent variables (intention, belief, strategy) from behavioral traces without active rival model discrimination.
